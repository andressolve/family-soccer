April — Foundations

Weeks 1–4

Build comfort on the ball. Every session follows the same rhythm: warm-up, main skill work, scrimmage, cool-down. Keep it fun — at 7 and 9, enjoyment = repetition = improvement.

Week 1 Saturday, April 4

Ball Mastery

Bring: 1 ball per person, 8 cones (or water bottles), 1 whistle

Warm-Up 10 min

Jog & Freeze
Everyone dribbles their ball freely inside a 15m x 15m square (mark corners with cones). When you blow the whistle, everyone stops the ball dead under the sole of their foot. Blow again to restart.
  1. Set up a square with 4 cones, roughly 15 big steps per side
  2. Everyone starts inside with a ball at their feet
  3. Dribble gently — try not to bump into each other
  4. Whistle = freeze! Stop the ball under your sole. Last one to stop does 3 jumping jacks
Toe Taps
Stand behind the ball. Alternate tapping the top of the ball lightly with the sole of each foot — left, right, left, right — like running in place on top of the ball. The ball should barely move.
  1. Ball is still on the ground in front of you
  2. Tap the top of the ball with the bottom of your right foot, then immediately switch to your left
  3. Stay on the balls of your feet (bouncy), keep a rhythm
  4. Do 30 seconds, rest 10, repeat. Count how many taps!
Watch for: the ball rolling away. It should stay in the same spot. If it moves, they're pushing too hard — lighter touches.
Side-to-Side Sole Rolls
Place sole of one foot on top of the ball and roll it sideways to the other foot. The other foot stops it, then rolls it back. Think of it like rolling a rolling pin side to side.
  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, ball between your feet
  2. Place the sole of your right foot on top of the ball
  3. Roll the ball to the left using the bottom of your foot
  4. Stop it with the sole of your left foot, then roll it back
  5. Keep your body balanced and upright — don't lean
Watch for: using the toes instead of the sole. The bottom/middle of the foot should be on top of the ball.

Main Drills 20 min

Sole Rolls — Forward & Back
Same idea as the warm-up sole rolls but now rolling the ball forward and backward (not sideways). Place sole on top of ball, roll it forward about 1 step, then drag it back toward you.
  1. Stand with ball in front of your right foot
  2. Place sole on top of ball, push it gently forward about 1 step
  3. Without lifting your foot off the ball, drag it back toward you
  4. Do 10 with right foot, then 10 with left foot
  5. Now try moving forward while doing it: push-roll forward, drag back, take a step, repeat
Watch for: the ball escaping. They should keep gentle downward pressure — not stamping on the ball, but not so light it slips away.
Inside-Outside Touches
Tap the ball back and forth between your feet while standing still, then while walking forward. Use the inside of each foot to push the ball to the other foot. Think of it like a little zig-zag between your feet.
  1. Ball between your feet, standing still
  2. Use the INSIDE of your right foot (the arch area) to tap the ball toward your left foot
  3. Use the INSIDE of your left foot to tap it back
  4. Keep a steady rhythm — tap, tap, tap, tap
  5. Once comfortable, start walking forward slowly while doing it
  6. Add the outside of the foot: tap with inside, then push with outside of the same foot to redirect
Watch for: big heavy touches that send the ball too far. Touches should be soft — the ball stays within reach at all times.
Ball Mastery Square
Set up a 5m x 5m square with cones. Each side of the square, you do a different touch. This gives structure and makes them practice multiple skills in one drill.
  1. Set up a small square (5 big steps per side) with 4 cones
  2. Side 1: Sole rolls forward — roll ball with bottom of foot as you walk along
  3. Side 2: Inside-outside touches — zig-zag the ball between your feet
  4. Side 3: Toe taps — do toe taps while moving sideways along the line
  5. Side 4: Free choice — pick whatever touch you want
  6. Go around 3 times, try to get faster each lap

Scrimmage 15 min

2v2 with 3-Touch Rule
A mini game! Two teams of two in a small area with small goals (use cones 1.5m apart for goals). The twist: before you can pass, you must take at least 3 touches. This forces them to dribble and use what they just learned.
  1. Mark a small pitch: about 15m x 10m. Put 2 cone goals at each end (each goal = 2 cones about 1.5m apart)
  2. Teams: parents vs kids OR one parent + one kid per team (mix it up)
  3. Rule: you must take at least 3 touches before you can pass or shoot
  4. No goalkeepers — score by rolling the ball through the cone goal on the ground
  5. Play to 5 goals, then switch teams

Cool-Down 5 min

Juggling Challenge
Drop the ball from your hands onto your foot and kick it back up. Count how many times you can keep it in the air. This is HARD at first — even 1 is great for a 7-year-old!
  1. Hold ball in both hands at waist height
  2. Drop it (don't throw!) and kick it back up with the top of your foot (laces area)
  3. Catch it. That's 1! Try for 2, then 3...
  4. Write down everyone's personal best — we'll try to beat it each week
Watch for: pointing toes down too much. The foot should be slightly angled so the ball goes up, not forward. Lock the ankle.
Stretching Circle
Everyone in a circle. Each person picks one stretch and everyone copies. Hold each stretch for 15 seconds: hamstrings (touch toes), quads (pull foot behind you), calves (push against wall or lean on each other), and hip flexors (big lunge step).
Coaching tip: Don't correct too much in Week 1. Let them explore the ball. Praise effort, not outcomes. "Great try!" beats "No, not like that." Sebastian may want to use his hands — let him do a short GK warm-up at the end if he asks.
Week 2 Saturday, April 11

Dribbling Basics

Bring: 1 ball per person, 10–12 cones, whistle

Warm-Up 10 min

Free Dribble in Grid
Same 15m x 15m square as Week 1. Everyone dribbles around inside, trying to avoid bumping into each other. This teaches awareness — looking up while dribbling.
  1. Set up 15m x 15m square
  2. Everyone dribbles their own ball inside the square
  3. Try not to hit anyone else or let your ball leave the square
  4. If your ball goes out, do 5 toe taps before coming back in
Traffic Light Game
Coach calls out colours while everyone dribbles. GREEN = dribble fast. YELLOW = dribble slow. RED = stop ball under sole. Kids love this one!
  1. Same grid. Everyone dribbling
  2. Shout "GREEN!" — dribble as fast as you can while keeping control
  3. Shout "YELLOW!" — slow down, tiny touches
  4. Shout "RED!" — stop the ball dead under your sole, freeze your body
  5. Mix it up randomly. Speed up the calls to make it harder
Watch for: when they go fast, the ball gets away from them. Emphasize "the ball stays close, like it's on a leash."

Main Drills 20 min

Cone Weave
Set up a line of 6 cones in a straight row, about 1.5m apart. Dribble in and out through the cones like a slalom skier. This teaches close ball control and using different parts of your foot.
  1. Line up 6 cones in a straight line, about 1.5m apart (roughly 2 big steps between each)
  2. Start at one end with the ball
  3. Round 1: Weave through using ONLY the inside of your foot (the arch) — push the ball gently around each cone
  4. Jog back to start. Round 2: use ONLY the outside of your foot — this is harder! Push with the pinky-toe side
  5. Round 3: alternate — inside around one cone, outside around the next
  6. For Sebastian: wider spacing (2m). For Francisco: tighter (1m) to challenge him
Watch for: kicking the ball ahead and chasing it. The ball should stay within arm's reach at all times. Encourage small, gentle taps.
Close Control Relay
A race! Dribble to a cone 10m away and back. But the ball must stay close — if it gets more than an arm's length away, you stop and pull it back before continuing. Speed + control.
  1. Place a cone 10m away (about 12 big steps)
  2. Two players line up at the start line with their balls
  3. On "GO!" — dribble to the cone, go around it, dribble back
  4. Rule: if the ball gets more than 1 arm's length from your foot, stop and bring it back before continuing
  5. Do best of 3 races. Winner picks the next warm-up game
Shadow Dribble
One person is the "leader" and dribbles wherever they want — changing speed, stopping, turning. The other person follows behind and copies everything they do. Then switch roles.
  1. Pairs: each pair has a ball (only the leader has the ball at first)
  2. Leader dribbles anywhere in the grid — slow, fast, stop, turn, change direction
  3. Follower stays 2 steps behind and copies every move (without a ball at first)
  4. After 2 minutes, switch leader/follower
  5. Challenge version: both have a ball — leader dribbles, follower copies while also controlling their own ball

Scrimmage 15 min

2v2 with Dribble Bonus
Same setup as Week 1 (small pitch, cone goals). Regular game BUT dribbling past a defender earns a bonus point even if you don't score. This encourages them to try dribbling, not just kicking the ball.
  1. Same small pitch as Week 1 (15m x 10m, cone goals)
  2. Goal = 1 point. Dribbling past a defender = 1 bonus point
  3. No goalkeepers — score through the cones on the ground
  4. Play to 10 points (counting both goals and dribble bonuses)

Cool-Down 5 min

King of the Ring
Everyone dribbles inside a circle (use the grid). While protecting your own ball, try to kick everyone else's ball out of the area. If your ball leaves, you're out. Last one standing wins! Great end-of-session game.
  1. Use the 15m x 15m grid (or make it smaller for more chaos)
  2. Everyone dribbles their ball inside
  3. While dribbling, try to kick OTHER people's balls out of the square
  4. If YOUR ball leaves the square, you're eliminated
  5. Last person with their ball still in the square wins!
  6. Parents: go easy but not too easy. Make it competitive enough to be fun
Coaching tip: "Head up!" is the #1 cue this week. Try this trick: while they dribble toward you, hold up 1, 2, or 3 fingers and ask them how many you're showing — forces them to look up! Keep the cone spacing wide for Sebastian (2m), tighter for Francisco (1m).
Week 3 Saturday, April 18

Passing

Bring: 1 ball per pair, 10–12 cones, whistle

Warm-Up 10 min

Pairs Passing — Increasing Distance
Two people face each other, about 3m apart (4 big steps). Pass the ball back and forth using the inside of your foot. After 10 good passes, take one step back. Keep going until it becomes hard to reach each other.
  1. Pair up (parent + kid). Stand 3m apart facing each other
  2. Pass using the INSIDE of your foot — the flat arch area, NOT your toe
  3. Plant your non-kicking foot next to the ball, pointing at your partner
  4. Swing kicking foot through the ball, striking with the inside arch
  5. Follow through toward your target (your foot points where the ball goes)
  6. After 10 clean passes, each person takes 1 step back. How far can you get?
Watch for: toe kicks! Kids naturally kick with their toe. Gently correct: "Use the side of your foot, like a hockey stick hitting a puck." Also watch for floppy ankles — the ankle should be locked firm.

Main Drills 20 min

Gate Passing
Scatter 6–8 mini "gates" around the grid (each gate = 2 cones about 1m apart). In pairs, try to pass the ball THROUGH as many gates as possible in 2 minutes. One person passes through the gate, the other receives on the other side.
  1. Set up 6–8 gates randomly inside the grid (each gate = 2 cones, about 1m apart)
  2. Pairs stand on opposite sides of any gate
  3. Pass through the gate to your partner. If it goes through cleanly, that's 1 point!
  4. After a successful pass, both run to a different gate and try again
  5. Set a timer: 2 minutes. Count your points. Try to beat your score in round 2
Watch for: rushing. Accuracy matters more than speed. "Slow is smooth, smooth is fast."
Triangle Passing
Three people stand in a triangle, about 5m apart. Pass and follow: pass the ball to the next person, then jog to their spot. The ball goes around the triangle while everyone keeps moving. This teaches "pass and move" — the most important habit in soccer.
  1. 3 players (or use a cone as the 4th "player") form a triangle, 5m sides
  2. Player A passes to Player B
  3. After passing, Player A JOGS to Player B's spot
  4. Player B receives, passes to Player C, then jogs to C's spot
  5. Keep the pattern going — pass, then move to where you passed
  6. Try to keep it smooth and flowing. Speed up gradually
Watch for: standing still after passing. The whole point is MOVE after you pass. "Pass and GO, never pass and WATCH."
Wall Pass Intro
The "wall pass" or "give-and-go" is the most useful move in soccer. Player A passes to Player B (the "wall"), Player B immediately passes it back, and Player A has run forward to receive it in space. Like using a teammate as a wall to bounce the ball off.
  1. A parent stands still about 5m in front of a child (the parent is the "wall")
  2. Child passes ball to parent with inside of foot
  3. IMMEDIATELY after passing, child sprints forward past the parent
  4. Parent passes the ball forward into the space ahead of the running child
  5. Child receives the ball on the run and shoots at a cone goal
  6. Key phrase: "Pass it, then SPRINT!" — don't wait to see what happens, go!

Scrimmage 15 min

2v2 — Pass to Score
Same small pitch. The rule: a goal only counts if the scorer received a pass before shooting. You can't just dribble and score — you MUST pass to a teammate first. This forces them to use what they learned today.
  1. Same pitch as previous weeks
  2. A goal only counts if it came from a pass (not dribbling in solo)
  3. Sebastian can go in goal for the last 5 minutes if he wants
  4. Play to 5 goals

Cool-Down 5 min

Longest Pass Challenge
One at a time, everyone tries to make the longest accurate pass. Put a cone as a target. Step further back each round. Who can hit the target from the farthest distance?
Coaching tip: "Lock your ankle, toe up" — the inside-foot pass needs a firm ankle. Demo the wrong way (floppy ankle, ball goes weakly sideways) vs. right way (locked ankle, firm clean pass). Kids this age naturally use their toe — gently redirect to the inside of the foot every time. Don't get frustrated if it takes weeks. It will click.
Week 4 Saturday, April 25

Receiving & First Touch

Bring: 1 ball per pair, 8 cones, whistle

Warm-Up 10 min

Toss & Receive
In pairs, one person gently tosses the ball (underhand, waist height) to the other, who must control it with their body before it bounces more than once. Try controlling with different body parts: inside of foot, sole, thigh.
  1. Pairs face each other, about 3m apart
  2. Player A holds ball and underhand-tosses it gently to Player B (waist height)
  3. Player B controls it with inside of foot — let the ball hit your foot, then PULL your foot back slightly to cushion it (like catching an egg — soft!)
  4. The ball should land gently at your feet, not bounce away
  5. 5 reps with inside of foot, then 5 with the sole (step on it as it bounces), then 5 with the thigh (let it hit mid-thigh and drop to your feet)
Watch for: stiff legs. The receiving foot/thigh needs to be RELAXED and give way on contact. Think: "catching an egg, not kicking a wall."

Main Drills 20 min

Pass & Control — Two Touch
In pairs, pass the ball firmly to each other. The receiver gets exactly 2 touches: Touch 1 = control the ball (stop it or redirect it). Touch 2 = pass it back. The goal is a clean first touch that sets up a clean pass.
  1. Pairs, 5m apart, facing each other
  2. Player A passes firmly (not gently!) with inside of foot
  3. Player B: Touch 1 — cushion the ball with inside of foot to stop it or push it slightly to the side
  4. Player B: Touch 2 — pass it back
  5. Key coaching: the first touch should move the ball into a good position for the pass. Don't just stop it dead — guide it to where you want it
  6. Do 10 each, then try to do it faster
Watch for: the first touch bouncing too far away. "Soft feet!" — pull the foot back on contact like a cushion absorbing the impact.
Turn & Go
The receiver has their BACK to the passer. They receive the ball, turn with it, and dribble through a cone gate. This is a game-realistic skill — you often receive the ball facing the wrong way and need to turn.
  1. Set up: a cone gate (2 cones, 1.5m apart) about 5m in front of the receiver
  2. Receiver stands with their back to the passer, facing the cone gate
  3. Passer calls "TURN!" and passes the ball to the receiver's feet
  4. Receiver lets the ball come to one side, takes a touch to control it, turns, and dribbles through the gate
  5. Try turning both ways: touch the ball to the left and spin left, or touch right and spin right
  6. Peek over your shoulder BEFORE the ball arrives to know which way to turn — this is called "checking your shoulder"
Watch for: not looking before receiving. Encourage peeking over the shoulder. In a real game, this tells you where the defenders are.
Bounce Game
Player A tosses the ball in the air toward Player B. Player B must control it with any body part BEFORE it bounces a second time. Toss it at different heights and angles to make it challenging.
  1. Pairs, 4m apart
  2. Player A tosses ball in the air (varies height: low, medium, high)
  3. The ball bounces once. Player B must control it before it bounces a SECOND time
  4. Control with whatever works: foot, thigh, chest (chest = let it hit you and drop to your feet)
  5. Score: clean control = 1 point. Control + able to pass it back = 2 points
  6. Play to 10 points then switch roles

Scrimmage 15 min

3v1 Keep-Away (Rondo)
3 players form a triangle and try to keep the ball away from 1 defender in the middle. The 3 outside players pass to each other while the defender tries to intercept. If the defender wins the ball, they swap with whoever made the bad pass. This is the single best small-group drill in soccer — Barcelona uses it every day.
  1. Mark a circle or triangle area, about 5m sides
  2. 3 players on the outside, 1 defender in the middle
  3. Outside players pass to each other — the defender tries to intercept or touch the ball
  4. If the defender touches the ball, they swap with whoever made the bad pass
  5. Outside players: max 2 touches (one to control, one to pass)
  6. Rotate the defender every 90 seconds regardless so nobody gets stuck
Watch for: outside players standing still. They should be moving to create passing angles — always giving their teammate a "window" to pass through.

Cool-Down 5 min

Juggling Challenge — Beat Last Week
Same as Week 1. Drop from hands, kick up, catch. Try to beat your personal record from last week. Track everyone's progress!
Coaching tip: "Soft feet!" — the ball should land like it's hitting a pillow, not a wall. The key cue is to pull the foot back slightly on contact. For Francisco, add the challenge of controlling while moving. Sebastian can practice catching (GK receiving) — same principle of soft hands. Praise any first touch that stays within reach, even if it's not perfect.

Build on the fundamentals. Introduce shooting, moves, defending, and Sebastian's goalkeeping. Sessions get more game-like as confidence grows.

Week 5 Saturday, May 2

Shooting & Goalkeeping Intro GK Session

Bring: 2–3 balls, 6 cones (for goal + targets), GK gloves for Sebastian if he has them (optional)

Warm-Up 10 min

Passing Pairs + Dynamic Stretches
Quick passing pairs (inside of foot, 10 each side) to get warmed up. Then dynamic stretches: high knees, butt kicks, side shuffles, and leg swings (swing leg forward and back, holding onto a partner for balance). These stretches prep the legs for shooting.

Main Drills — Shooting 10 min

Laces Strike Technique
Shooting uses the top of your foot (laces area), not the inside or toe. The key is the plant foot position and following through.
  1. Place the ball still on the ground. Stand 2 steps behind it
  2. Step forward and plant your non-kicking foot RIGHT BESIDE the ball, toes pointing at the target
  3. Swing your kicking leg through, striking the MIDDLE of the ball with your LACES (top of foot where the shoelaces are)
  4. Point your toes DOWN and lock your ankle firm
  5. Follow through — your kicking leg should swing all the way up after contact
  6. Land on your kicking foot (you'll naturally hop forward slightly)
Watch for: planting the foot too far behind the ball (causes sky-high shots) or too far to the side (causes the ball to slice sideways). Plant foot should be RIGHT NEXT to the ball. Also: toe kicks — remind them "laces, not toes!"
Target Practice
Set up a goal with cones (about 3m wide). Place a cone inside each bottom corner — these are the targets. Take turns shooting from 8m away (about 10 big steps). Score points for hitting the targets.
  1. Make a goal: 2 tall cones 3m apart. Place 1 target cone in each bottom corner
  2. Shooting line at 8m (10 big steps away)
  3. Take turns: 5 shots each. 1 point = on target (would go in). 3 points = hit a corner cone!
  4. Try from different angles: straight on, left side, right side
  5. Reminder: "Your plant foot points where you want the ball to go"

Main Drills — Goalkeeping 10 min

Ready Position
The goalkeeper's starting stance before every save. This is the foundation of all goalkeeping.
  1. Stand in the middle of the goal, facing the ball
  2. Feet shoulder-width apart, weight on the balls of your feet (not your heels)
  3. Knees slightly bent — like you're about to sit on a high stool
  4. Hands up at waist height, palms facing forward, fingers spread
  5. Lean slightly forward — you should feel like you could spring in any direction
  6. Practice: hold this position, and on "GO!" shuffle 2 steps left, set, then 2 steps right, set
"W" Hands Catching
For balls at chest height and above, catch with both hands making a "W" shape behind the ball. Thumbs almost touching, fingers spread wide. The ball sits in the web of your fingers, not your palms.
  1. Hold your hands up in front of your face, thumbs almost touching, fingers spread
  2. Look at the shape your thumbs and index fingers make — it's a "W"
  3. This is how you catch any ball above your waist
  4. Practice: partner tosses ball gently to chest height. Catch with W-hands, pull ball into your chest
  5. For LOW balls (ground level): scoop — get down on one knee, hands scoop under the ball, bring it into your body
  6. Do 5 high catches, 5 low scoops. Alternate
Watch for: trying to clap the ball between palms. Fingers should be BEHIND the ball, not on the sides. And always pull the ball into your body after catching — never leave it out in front.
Basic Shot-Stopping
Gentle shots rolled along the ground from 5m away. GK gets set in ready position, then saves. Everyone takes turns in goal, but Sebastian gets extra reps.
  1. GK stands in goal in ready position
  2. Shooter stands 5m away and ROLLS the ball firmly along the ground (not kicking yet)
  3. GK gets down and scoops the ball — get your body BEHIND the ball, not just hands
  4. After 5 rolled saves, progress to gentle kicked shots along the ground
  5. Then try shots at different heights: ground, waist, chest
  6. Everyone takes 5 turns in goal. Sebastian gets 10

Scrimmage 15 min

2v1 + GK
Two attackers try to score against one defender with Sebastian in goal. Great because it combines shooting, passing, and goalkeeping from today's session.
  1. Full-width goal (or cone goal about 4m wide)
  2. Sebastian in goal. One parent as defender
  3. Two attackers (one kid + one parent, or both kids) start 15m out
  4. Attackers try to score. Defender tries to stop them
  5. Rotate roles every 3 minutes so everyone plays every position
Coaching tip: Shooting — "your plant foot points where you want the ball to go." GK — never force diving yet, focus on getting the BODY behind the ball. The hands just help. Sebastian: praise him for making himself big and staying on his feet. "Great position!" is the best compliment for a GK.
Week 6 Saturday, May 9

1v1 Moves

Bring: 1 ball per person, 8–10 cones, 4 cones for mini goals

Warm-Up 10 min

Ball Mastery Review + Cone Gates at Speed
2 minutes of toe taps, sole rolls, inside-outside touches to wake up the feet. Then dribble through scattered cone gates (1m wide) as fast as possible — how many gates can you get through in 1 minute?

Main Drills 20 min

Step-Over
A fake move to trick a defender. You swing your foot OVER the ball (without touching it), then push the ball the other direction with the OUTSIDE of your foot. The defender thinks you're going one way, but you go the other.
  1. Place ball on the ground. Stand behind it
  2. Swing your RIGHT foot in a big circle OVER the top of the ball, from inside to outside (your foot goes over without touching it)
  3. Plant that right foot on the ground on the other side of the ball
  4. Immediately push the ball to the LEFT with the OUTSIDE of your LEFT foot
  5. Accelerate away! The move is useless without speed after it
  6. Practice stationary first (just the foot motion). Then try it while walking. Then at a cone (pretend the cone is a defender)
Watch for: stepping ON the ball instead of over it. Also: no acceleration after — the move only works if you EXPLODE away after the fake.
Scissors
Similar to the step-over but the foot goes around the ball from OUTSIDE to INSIDE. Then you push the ball the opposite way. It's a bit flashier and makes defenders freeze.
  1. Ball on the ground. Dribble slowly toward a cone
  2. Swing your RIGHT foot around the ball from OUTSIDE to INSIDE (the opposite direction of a step-over)
  3. Your foot wraps around the front of the ball without touching it
  4. Plant your right foot. Push ball to the RIGHT with the OUTSIDE of your LEFT foot
  5. Speed away!
  6. Start slow. It feels awkward at first. That's normal. Try 10 on each side
Watch for: the fake not being convincing. Drop your shoulder and lean your body in the fake direction — sell it! The body fake matters more than the foot.
Body Feint
The simplest and most effective move. Drop your shoulder and lean your body hard to one side (like you're about to go that way), then push the ball the other direction. No fancy footwork needed — just "sell the fake."
  1. Dribble toward a cone
  2. About 2m before the cone, drop your LEFT shoulder hard to the left — lean your whole upper body left like you're about to sprint that way
  3. The cone-defender "bites" on the fake
  4. Immediately push the ball to the RIGHT with the outside of your right foot
  5. Sprint past the cone on the right side
  6. Key: make the fake big and dramatic. The bigger the fake, the better it works
1v1 Channel
Now use the moves against a real person! Set up a narrow channel (2m wide, 10m long) with cones. Attacker tries to dribble past defender and out the other end. Defender tries to stop them or kick the ball out of the channel.
  1. Set up a channel: 2 lines of cones, 2m apart, 10m long
  2. Attacker starts at one end with the ball. Defender at the other end
  3. Attacker's goal: dribble past the defender and out the far end
  4. Defender's goal: block the ball or kick it out of the channel
  5. Attacker should try using the moves from today: step-over, scissors, or body feint
  6. Each person attacks 5 times, then switch. Keep score!

Scrimmage 15 min

1v1 to Mini Goals — Must Use a Move
Two mini goals (cone goals, 1.5m wide). 1v1: you can only score if you use a move (step-over, scissors, or feint) first. Winner stays on, loser swaps with someone waiting.

Cool-Down 5 min

Freestyle Show-Off
Each person shows their favourite move from today. Everyone claps. Sebastian can show a GK save if he prefers. Stretching to finish.
Coaching tip: Kids LOVE this session — it's the one they'll practice all week. Let them be creative and don't worry about perfect technique. The key coaching point is change of pace: the move is useless without ACCELERATION after. "Fake it, then EXPLODE!" For Sebastian: let him be the defender sometimes — reading the attacker's body language helps his GK anticipation.
Week 7 Saturday, May 16

Change of Direction

Bring: 1 ball per person, 10–12 cones

Warm-Up 10 min

Dribble & Cut on Whistle
Everyone dribbles in the grid. On the whistle, immediately change direction 90 degrees using any cut. This teaches quick reactions while dribbling.

Main Drills 20 min

Inside Cut
The most basic direction change. You're dribbling forward and need to go left or right. Use the INSIDE of your foot to chop the ball sideways.
  1. Dribble toward a cone
  2. About 1m before the cone, plant your LEFT foot beside the ball
  3. Use the INSIDE of your RIGHT foot to push the ball sharply to the LEFT (across your body)
  4. Turn your body and accelerate in the new direction
  5. The ball should change direction about 90 degrees
  6. Practice on both sides: inside of right foot to go left, inside of left foot to go right
Outside Hook
You're dribbling forward and want to REVERSE direction (180 degrees). Hook the ball with the outside of your foot and spin back the way you came.
  1. Dribble toward a cone
  2. About 1m before the cone, reach your RIGHT foot forward and wrap it around the front of the ball
  3. Using the OUTSIDE of your foot (pinky-toe side), drag the ball back toward you in a U-turn
  4. Spin your body 180 degrees and dribble back the way you came
  5. It's like doing a U-turn in a car — the ball curves around
Cruyff Turn
Named after Dutch legend Johan Cruyff. You fake a pass or shot, then instead of kicking the ball, you drag it behind your standing leg and go the opposite direction. It's beautiful when done right — and very effective against defenders.
  1. Dribble toward a cone. Shape your body as if you're about to pass or shoot — swing your kicking leg back
  2. Instead of kicking, use the INSIDE of your kicking foot to tap the ball BEHIND your standing leg
  3. The ball goes behind you and to the other side of your body
  4. Turn and sprint after the ball in the new direction
  5. Break it down: practice the drag-behind-the-leg motion standing still first. Ball in front of right foot. Inside of right foot taps it behind left leg
  6. Then add the fake: shape to shoot... drag behind... turn and go!
Watch for: not selling the fake. The whole point is the defender thinks you're about to pass/shoot. Big backswing with the leg before dragging behind. Also, the ball should go BEHIND the standing leg, not just sideways.
Cone Gauntlet
Set up 6 cones in a zig-zag. At each cone, perform a specific turn: inside cut at cone 1, outside hook at cone 2, Cruyff at cone 3, and repeat. Continuous — no stopping between cones.

Scrimmage 15 min

2v2 Escape Game
No goals! Instead, you score by dribbling the ball over the opponent's end line (like American football). This forces LOTS of direction changes because you need to evade defenders and can score along the entire width of the line.
  1. 15m x 10m pitch. No goals — just end lines
  2. Score by dribbling the ball over the other team's end line
  3. The ball must be under control when you cross (not a big kick)
  4. This forces dribbling, direction changes, and using moves from today

Cool-Down 5 min

Cruyff Turn Speed Challenge
Who can do 10 Cruyff turns fastest? Time each person. This will be messy and funny — that's fine! Stretching to finish.
Coaching tip: The Cruyff turn will take many attempts. Break it down: 1) shape to pass, 2) drag ball behind standing leg, 3) turn and go. Francisco may get it faster — have him demo for Sebastian. Use the phrase "show and go" to cue the fake. Don't worry if it's sloppy — they'll refine it over weeks of practice.
Week 8 Saturday, May 23

Goalkeeping Deep Dive Sebastian's Session

Bring: 2–3 balls, GK gloves for Sebastian (optional), 6 cones for goal, soft grass location if possible (for diving practice)

Warm-Up 10 min

GK Lateral Shuffles
Set up 2 cones 4m apart on the goal line. GK shuffles sideways between them, touching each cone. Never cross your feet — always stay facing forward in ready position.
  1. 2 cones on the goal line, 4m apart
  2. GK starts at one cone in ready position
  3. Shuffle sideways to the other cone (side-step, don't cross feet)
  4. Touch the cone, set position, shuffle back
  5. Do 10 trips. Stay low, stay bouncy
Catch & Throw Pairs
Everyone pairs up. Toss balls to each other at different heights: high (head height, W-catch), medium (chest, pull into body), low (ground, scoop). 5 each height.
Reaction Game
GK in ready position. Coach points left or right. GK shuffles 2 steps that direction and sets. Coach immediately tosses ball — GK saves. This trains reaction and recovery.

Main Drills — Everyone 10 min

Shot-Stopping Circuit
Everyone takes turns in goal: 5 shots each from 8m. Mix up the shots: ground level, waist height, to the corners. The GK tries to save using proper technique from Week 5 (W-hands, scoop, ready position).
GK Distribution
After saving, the GK needs to get the ball to a teammate. Practice two methods: the roll (bowling motion, for short distances) and the overarm throw (like a cricket throw, for longer distances). Roll to nearby players, throw to far players.
  1. GK saves a shot, holds the ball
  2. A teammate stands 5m away. GK rolls the ball to them (bowling motion, keep it on the ground)
  3. Next: teammate stands 10m away. GK throws overarm (step forward with opposite foot, throw over shoulder)
  4. Accuracy matters more than distance. Hit the teammate's feet!

Main Drills — Sebastian Focus 10 min

Diving Intro — Progressive Steps
Diving is built up gradually. Never start from standing — work up from kneeling to crouching to standing. Always on soft grass.
  1. STEP 1 — Kneeling: Sebastian kneels on both knees. Roll the ball 1m to his left. He falls sideways and catches it, landing on his side. 5 each side
  2. STEP 2 — Crouching: Start in a low squat. Roll ball 1.5m to the side. Push off with the closer foot and fall sideways to save. 5 each side
  3. STEP 3 — Standing: If he's comfortable, try from standing. Gentle roll to the side. Push off, dive, land on side. Only if he's ready!
  4. Key: always land on your SIDE, not flat on your front. Tuck the bottom arm in
  5. Always use rolled balls first, not kicked. Kicked balls come later
Watch for: fear. If he's hesitant, stay at the kneeling stage longer. Build confidence before progressing. Praise every attempt. "That was brave!" is the best thing to say.
1v1 Bravery — Narrowing the Angle
When an attacker dribbles toward goal, the GK should come OFF the line toward the ball. This makes the goal look smaller to the attacker. Teach Sebastian to be brave and come out.
  1. Sebastian stands on the goal line
  2. Francisco starts 15m out and dribbles toward goal
  3. As Francisco gets closer, Sebastian walks OUT toward him — arms spread wide, making himself BIG
  4. Sebastian should close the distance but NOT commit — stay on his feet, stay balanced
  5. When Francisco shoots, Sebastian is closer to the ball and has a better chance
  6. Key phrase: "Come out, get big, stay on your feet"
GK Footwork — No Crossed Feet
Goalkeepers shuffle along the goal line — they should NEVER cross their feet because it makes them lose balance. Practice shuffling while staying in ready position.
  1. Sebastian on the goal line in ready position
  2. Coach points left — Sebastian shuffles left (left foot steps left, right foot follows — never cross)
  3. Coach points right — shuffles right
  4. After each shuffle, set feet and get in ready position before next call
  5. Then add a shot after the shuffle — shuffle, set, SAVE!

Scrimmage 15 min

2v1 + GK — "Save 5 in a Row" Challenge
Sebastian in goal. Two attackers vs. one defender. Challenge: can Sebastian save 5 shots in a row? Francisco practices shooting, Sebastian practices saving. Everyone cheers.
Coaching tip: This is Sebastian's day to shine. Make him feel like the expert. Key cues: "Set your feet before the shot," "hands up, ready," and "attack the ball, don't wait for it." For diving — ALWAYS onto soft grass, NEVER force it. Let him build confidence at his own pace. Use rolled balls before kicked ones. If he's nervous about diving, that's totally okay — stay at catching standing up. The bravery will come with time.
Week 9 Saturday, May 30

Defending Basics

Bring: 1 ball per person, 8 cones

Warm-Up 10 min

Tag with Balls
Everyone dribbles a ball. One person is "it" and has to tag someone while dribbling their own ball. If tagged, you become "it." This teaches dribbling under pressure and spatial awareness — basically defending and attacking at the same time.
Shadow Defending
No ball. Pairs face each other. The "attacker" moves side to side, forward, back. The "defender" mirrors their movements — always staying directly in front of them. This teaches body positioning without the ball to distract.
  1. Pairs face each other, 1m apart, no ball
  2. Attacker moves freely: left, right, forward, back
  3. Defender stays facing attacker and mirrors every movement — always directly between attacker and an imaginary goal behind the defender
  4. Defender's stance: sideways on (one foot forward, one back), low, balanced, on balls of feet
  5. 30 seconds, then switch roles

Main Drills 20 min

Jockeying
The defensive stance: instead of charging at the attacker, the defender stays on their feet, stays low, and shuffles backward while keeping their body between the attacker and the goal. The goal is to DELAY the attacker, not to win the ball immediately.
  1. Defender faces attacker. Attacker has the ball
  2. Defender's body position: turned SIDEWAYS (one shoulder pointing at the attacker). Knees bent, low centre of gravity
  3. As the attacker dribbles forward, the defender shuffles BACKWARDS, always staying about 1m away
  4. DON'T lunge in! DON'T turn your back! Just stay in front and shuffle
  5. Your job: slow them down and wait for them to make a mistake (a big touch, looking down)
  6. Practice: attacker dribbles 20m slowly. Defender jockeys the whole way without lunging
Watch for: standing upright (too easy to beat — stay LOW) and diving in (the #1 mistake young defenders make).
Channeling — "Show Them the Sideline"
Instead of blocking the attacker head-on, position your body to force them toward the sideline (where they're less dangerous). You angle your body so the only way they can go is toward the edge of the field.
  1. Attacker starts in the middle of the pitch with the ball
  2. Defender approaches at an angle — instead of running straight at them, come from slightly to one side
  3. Position your body so the attacker's only easy path is toward the sideline, not toward the goal
  4. Keep shuffling to maintain that angle — don't let them cut inside
  5. The attacker is forced to the sideline where they're less dangerous and more likely to lose the ball
  6. Coach calls out: "Show them the sideline!" as a reminder
Block Tackle
When the attacker makes a mistake (big touch, looks down), THAT is when you tackle. The block tackle uses the inside of your foot to block the ball as the attacker tries to dribble past.
  1. Timing is everything — only tackle when the ball is away from the attacker's foot
  2. Step in firmly and place the inside of your foot against the ball
  3. Lock your ankle (just like passing!) and lean your body into it
  4. The goal is to wedge the ball between your foot and theirs, stopping it dead
  5. Then poke it away or push through
  6. Practice: attacker dribbles slowly. Defender jockeys, waits for a big touch, then tackles
Watch for: reckless tackling (going in too hard). This is family soccer! Emphasize timing over force.
1v1 Defending — Closing Down
Attacker starts 10m away. Defender sprints toward them, then SLOWS DOWN at about 2m and gets into jockeying position. The sprint-then-slow is called "closing down" — you eat up the space fast, then get patient.
  1. Attacker stands still with ball. Defender starts 10m away
  2. On "GO!" defender sprints toward attacker
  3. At about 2m away, defender SLOWS DOWN and gets into sideways jockey stance
  4. Don't sprint all the way in — you'll fly past them! The slowdown is crucial
  5. Now jockey as the attacker tries to dribble past
  6. Practice the timing: sprint → brake → jockey → wait → tackle when the moment is right

Scrimmage 15 min

2v2 — Points for Winning the Ball
Regular 2v2 but the defending team earns a point for winning the ball AND completing 3 passes afterward. This rewards good defending AND composure after winning the ball. Sebastian in goal — coach him to shout directions to defenders: "Left side!" "Close him down!" "Stay on your feet!"

Cool-Down 5 min

Group Discussion + Stretching
Sit in a circle. Ask: "What's harder — attacking or defending? Why?" Let the kids talk. There's no wrong answer. Stretching to finish.
Coaching tip: "Stay on your feet!" is the mantra all week. Kids always dive in — teach patience. The defender wins by NOT getting beaten, not by winning the ball. Reward patience: "Great job staying on your feet!" For Sebastian in goal: this is where he learns to talk to his defenders — encourage him to shout instructions. A loud goalkeeper is a good goalkeeper.

Week 10 Saturday, June 6

Combination Play

Bring: 1 ball, 8 cones, bibs/pinnies if you have them (to tell teams apart)

Warm-Up 10 min

Triangle Passing — One Touch
Same triangle passing from Week 3 but now try to do it in ONE touch (pass without stopping the ball first). This is much harder! If one touch is too difficult, allow two. Pass and move to the spot you passed to.

Main Drills 20 min

Wall Pass (Give-and-Go) — Full Version
The wall pass from Week 3, now against a real defender. Player A passes to Player B, then sprints PAST the defender. Player B immediately plays the ball into the space where Player A is running. It's called "give-and-go" because you give the ball and GO.
  1. Set up: one "defender" (parent) standing still. Player A with ball, Player B to the side
  2. Player A dribbles toward the defender
  3. When close (about 3m), Player A passes to Player B (the "wall")
  4. IMMEDIATELY Player A sprints around the defender (either side)
  5. Player B plays the ball into the space AHEAD of where Player A is running
  6. Player A receives the ball on the run — they've now beaten the defender!
  7. Key: timing! The return pass from B must be played BEFORE A arrives. Pass it into space, not to their feet
Watch for: Player A stopping after the pass. "PASS AND GO! Don't pass and watch!" Also: Player B's return pass needs to be in FRONT of the runner, not at them.
Overlap Run
Player A has the ball wide (near the sideline). Player A passes to Player B who is slightly ahead. Player A then runs AROUND the outside of Player B (overlapping them). Player B passes to A who is now in a new position. This creates confusion for the defender — who do they mark?
  1. Player A on the sideline with ball. Player B slightly ahead and inside
  2. A passes to B
  3. A sprints forward around the OUTSIDE of B (between B and the sideline)
  4. B holds the ball for 1-2 seconds (to let A get ahead), then passes forward to A
  5. A receives the ball further up the field in space
  6. Practice both sides of the field
2v1 Attack
Two attackers vs. one defender. The attackers must use teamwork (wall pass, overlap, or simple passing) to get past the defender. The defender can't guard both players — so the attackers need to use the extra person.
  1. Two attackers start 15m from goal with 1 ball. One defender between them and the goal
  2. Attackers try to score. They outnumber the defender!
  3. Coach: ask "Who is the defender NOT watching? That's who should get the ball!"
  4. Try using a wall pass, or just a simple pass to the open teammate
  5. Rotate roles every 3 attempts

Scrimmage 15 min

2v2 + GK — Combination Goals Only
Goal only counts if it comes from a wall pass or overlap play. Sebastian in goal, practicing distribution (throws to teammates to start attacks). Can Sebastian start a combination play by throwing to a teammate?

Cool-Down 5 min

3v1 Rondo
Quick keep-away game from Week 4. 3 outside, 1 in the middle. Max 2 touches. Fast passing. Rotate defender every 60 seconds. Great for ending on a high — everyone loves rondos by now.
Coaching tip: The wall pass is a game-changer at this age because nobody expects it. Key cue: "pass and GO, don't pass and watch." Play with the kids — they need a reliable "wall" to pass off. Sebastian can practice GK distribution to start these combinations. This week is where all the skills come together!

Game Awareness Freeze (10 min)
Play a 2v2 game but the coach can shout "FREEZE!" at any moment. Everyone stops exactly where they are. Coach asks questions: "Where should you be? What are your options? Who is open?   6. Unfreeze and see if they make a better decision

Coaching tip: Less coaching, more playing today. Step back and let them figure things out. Only intervene with freeze moments. The questions are more important than the answers — "What did you see?" "Was there a better option?" Don't tell them what to do — ask them what they COULD do. Sebastian: let him play outfield too. Being well-rounded helps goalkeepers understand the game better.
Week 12 Saturday, June 20

Set Pieces & Game Situations

Bring: 2–3 balls, 6 cones for goal, whistle

Warm-Up 10 min

Shooting from Different Positions + GK Warm-Up
Take turns shooting from different spots: straight on (10m), left side (angle), right side (angle), and close range (5m). Sebastian in goal for the warm-up, getting ready for set-piece practice. 3 shots from each position per person.

Main Drills — Set Pieces 10 min

Corner Kicks
A corner kick happens when the defending team kicks the ball over their own end line. The attacking team places the ball in the corner arc and kicks it into the box. In family soccer, just getting the ball into the area is 80% of the job.
  1. Place ball at the corner of the pitch (in a real game it's in the corner arc)
  2. Kicker: use the inside of your foot to curve the ball into the area in front of the goal
  3. Aim for the area between the penalty spot and the goal — not directly at the goal
  4. Attacker: stand near the goal and TIME your run to meet the ball. Run ONTO the ball, don't stand still
  5. Try heading it or shooting with your foot on the volley (before it bounces)
  6. Sebastian: practice coming out to CATCH corners. Call "KEEPER!" loudly so everyone knows to get out of the way
Free Kicks
A free kick is awarded when the other team commits a foul. You get a free shot at goal from wherever the foul happened. Practice direct free kicks from about 12-15m out.
  1. Place ball 12m from goal (about 15 big steps)
  2. Pick your target: bottom corner is usually best — low and to the side
  3. Step back 3 steps. Deep breath. Eyes on your target, not the ball
  4. Approach and strike with laces or inside of foot
  5. Each person takes 5 free kicks. Who scores the most?
Throw-Ins
When the ball goes out on the sideline, the other team throws it back in. There are specific rules that are commonly broken.
  1. Both feet must stay ON THE GROUND (can't jump or lift a foot)
  2. Ball must come from BEHIND your head (bring it all the way back over)
  3. Use BOTH hands equally (can't throw with just one hand)
  4. You must throw from behind the sideline (can't step onto the field)
  5. Practice: 5 throw-ins each, aiming at a teammate's feet. Accuracy matters!

Main Drills — Decision Making 10 min

Pass or Dribble?
An attacker faces a defender. Behind the defender is a goal. A teammate stands to one side. The coach calls out a number: "1!" means 1v1 (just you vs defender — dribble!). "2!" means 2v1 (your teammate enters — pass!). The attacker must READ the situation and make the right choice.
  1. Attacker with ball, 12m from goal. Defender between them and the goal
  2. Teammate waits to the side
  3. Coach shouts "1!" — it's 1v1. Attacker should dribble and use a move to beat the defender
  4. Coach shouts "2!" — teammate runs on. Now it's 2v1. Attacker should PASS to the open teammate
  5. The attacker doesn't know which number is coming — they must react and decide
  6. Key lesson: when you have a teammate open, PASS. When you're alone, DRIBBLE
Transition Game
2v2 but with instant transitions: the moment you lose the ball, you're defending. The moment you win it back, you're attacking. No breaks! This teaches the mental switch that separates good players.
  1. 2v2 game, small pitch
  2. When your team loses the ball: IMMEDIATELY press the ball and try to win it back. Don't stop and think!
  3. When your team wins the ball: IMMEDIATELY look forward and attack. Don't waste time!
  4. Coach watches for players who react quickly to turnovers. Praise: "Great reaction when you lost it!"

Scrimmage 15 min

Full Game with Proper Set Pieces
Play a 2v2+GK game, but every time the ball goes out of play, practice the proper restart: throw-ins on the sideline, corners when the ball goes over the end line, free kicks for fouls. Sebastian practices positioning on corners and free kicks — where should the GK stand?
Coaching tip: Set pieces are free goals waiting to happen at youth level. For corners — just getting the ball into the box is 80% of the job. For Sebastian: teach him to claim crosses by calling "KEEPER!" loudly and coming out confidently — if the GK calls it, everyone else clears out. Decision-making drills (pass or dribble?) are the most important thing at this age — more valuable than any skill move.
